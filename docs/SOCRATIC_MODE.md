# Socratic Questions Mode - User Guide

## Overview

Socratic Questions mode generates provocative, thought-provoking questions that challenge your understanding and push you to think critically about the material. Unlike quiz questions that test what you know, these questions explore what's NOT explicitly stated in your notes.

## Two Modes Available

### 📄 Static Mode

View all generated questions at once without interaction.

**Use when:**
- You want to see all questions together
- You prefer to think about questions at your own pace
- You want to copy questions for later reflection

**How it works:**
1. AI generates 5-8 provocative questions
2. All questions are displayed in a single view
3. You can read, copy, or save them for later

### 💬 Interactive Mode

Answer questions one by one and receive AI evaluation with scores.

**Use when:**
- You want immediate feedback on your thinking
- You want to measure your understanding
- You want guided practice in critical thinking

**How it works:**
1. AI generates 5-8 provocative questions
2. Questions are presented one at a time
3. You type your answer (be thoughtful and detailed!)
4. AI evaluates your answer based on:
   - Depth of thinking
   - Critical thinking skills
   - Coherence of argumentation
   - Openness to uncertainty
   - Use of context from material
5. You receive a score (0-10) and detailed feedback
6. Move to next question or skip
7. At the end, view overall results with average score

## Question Types

Socratic questions are designed to:

1. **Challenge assumptions**
   - "You wrote X, but how does this reconcile with Y?"
   
2. **Explore contradictions**
   - "The source claims A, but doesn't address B - how might B change the conclusion?"
   
3. **Test boundaries with "what if" scenarios**
   - "What happens if the author's assumption about Z turns out to be false?"
   
4. **Question implications**
   - "If this theory is correct, what would that mean for...?"
   
5. **Probe gaps**
   - "The notes mention X but don't explain why - what could be the underlying reason?"
   
6. **Force reconciliation of viewpoints**
   - "How can both X and Y be true simultaneously?"

## Scoring System (Interactive Mode)

### Score Ranges

- **8-10 (Excellent)**: Deep critical thinking, challenges assumptions, coherent arguments
- **6-7 (Good)**: Solid thinking with some depth, mostly coherent
- **4-5 (Fair)**: Basic understanding but lacks depth or critical analysis
- **0-3 (Poor)**: Superficial or off-topic response

### What AI Evaluates

1. **Depth of Thinking**: How deeply you reflect on the question
2. **Critical Thinking**: Do you challenge assumptions or just accept them?
3. **Coherence**: Is your argument logical and well-structured?
4. **Openness to Uncertainty**: Do you acknowledge complexity and ambiguity?
5. **Context Usage**: Do you reference the source material appropriately?

## Tips for Better Answers

### ✅ Do:
- Take your time to think deeply
- Reference specific parts of the source material
- Acknowledge uncertainty and complexity
- Challenge assumptions (including your own)
- Explore multiple perspectives
- Use "what if" thinking
- Connect ideas across different sections

### ❌ Don't:
- Give one-sentence answers
- Simply restate what's in your notes
- Avoid the question
- Be overly confident without justification
- Ignore context from the source
- Rush through questions

## Example Workflow

### Interactive Mode Example

**Question 1:**
> "You noted that the author advocates for X, but the source doesn't address how this would work in scenario Y. What challenges might arise?"

**Good Answer:**
> "The author's advocacy for X assumes conditions A and B are present. In scenario Y, condition A might not hold because [reason]. This could lead to [consequence]. However, if we consider [alternative approach], it might be possible to adapt X by [modification]. The source doesn't address this, which suggests either the author hasn't considered edge cases, or scenario Y falls outside the intended scope."

**Score: 8/10**
- Deep thinking ✓
- Challenges assumptions ✓
- Considers alternatives ✓
- Acknowledges gaps ✓

**Poor Answer:**
> "I think it would work fine because the author is smart."

**Score: 2/10**
- No depth ✗
- No critical thinking ✗
- Doesn't engage with the question ✗

## Usage Instructions

1. **Open a note** with your reading notes
2. **Run command**: "Reading Coach: Socratic Questions"
3. **Enter source material** (URL or paste text)
4. **Choose mode**: Static or Interactive
5. **For Interactive Mode**:
   - Read each question carefully
   - Type a thoughtful answer (aim for 3-5 sentences minimum)
   - Submit and review evaluation
   - Learn from feedback
   - Continue to next question
6. **Review final results** (Interactive mode shows overall score)

## Settings

Configure in Settings → Reading Coach:

- **Enable/Disable Mode**: Toggle Socratic Questions on/off
- **Custom Prompts**: Customize question generation (English/Russian)
- **AI Provider**: Choose your preferred AI (affects evaluation quality)
- **Temperature**: Higher = more creative questions (default: 0.7)

## Language Support

- **Auto-detect**: Automatically detects language from your notes
- **English**: Full support
- **Russian**: Full support (Русский)
- Questions and evaluations are provided in the detected language

## Best Practices

1. **Use after initial note-taking**: Take notes first, then use Socratic mode
2. **Don't rush**: Quality over speed in Interactive mode
3. **Reflect on feedback**: Learn from AI evaluations
4. **Iterate**: Try answering again after reading feedback
5. **Combine modes**: Use Static mode for overview, Interactive for practice
6. **Save results**: Copy final results to a note for future reference

## Troubleshooting

**Q: Questions seem too easy/hard?**
- Adjust AI temperature in settings
- Try different AI providers (GPT-4 generates harder questions)
- Customize prompts to specify difficulty level

**Q: Evaluation seems unfair?**
- Remember: AI evaluates critical thinking, not correctness
- There are no "right" answers, only better-reasoned ones
- Review evaluation criteria above

**Q: Can't parse questions in Interactive mode?**
- AI response format may vary
- Try Static mode instead
- Check AI provider settings

**Q: Want to save my answers?**
- Copy from final results screen
- Take screenshots
- Paste into a new note for future reference

## Advanced Tips

### For Deeper Learning

1. **Answer twice**: First attempt, then after reading evaluation
2. **Compare with peers**: Discuss questions with study partners
3. **Create follow-up notes**: Turn questions into research topics
4. **Track progress**: Keep a log of scores over time
5. **Challenge the AI**: If you disagree with evaluation, reflect on why

### For Educators

- Use Interactive mode as assessment tool
- Review student scores and answers
- Identify common misconceptions
- Generate discussion topics from questions
- Customize prompts for specific learning objectives

## Privacy & Data

- Your answers are sent to AI provider for evaluation
- No data is stored permanently by the plugin
- Results are shown only in the modal (not saved automatically)
- To keep records, manually copy results to notes
